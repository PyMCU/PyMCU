**The AGPL-3.0 does NOT apply to the output produced by the PyMCU compiler.**

> The machine-code, assembly, hex images, and any other artefacts generated by running
> the PyMCU compiler on your Python source files are the result of your own creative
> work. They do **not** constitute a derivative work of the PyMCU compiler itself, and
> they are **not** subject to the AGPL-3.0 or any other license imposed by PyMCU.
> You retain full ownership of and may license your compiled firmware under any terms
> you choose.

This exemption mirrors the approach used by the GNU Compiler Collection (GCC) through
its "GCC Runtime Library Exception": using a copyleft compiler does not infect the
output of that compiler.
